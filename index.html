About us

IASA, accredited at level IV since 1997 by the Ukrainian Cabinet of Ministers, was formed through the merger of the Research Institute for Applied Systems Analysis, the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine, and the Ministry of Education of Ukraine, along with the Department of Mathematical Methods of System Analysis. IASA comprises three academic departments: Mathematical Methods of Systems Analysis, Applied Nonlinear Analysis, and numerical optimization methods. In 2006, it expanded to include the Department of Computer Aided Design (formerly known as the "Chair of Design and Production of Electronic Computing Equipment" until 1985, and later renamed as the "Systems Engineering Department") in collaboration with the Institute of Information Resources (INFORES).

Departments

System Analysis System Design Artificial Intelligence

The department trains specialists in system analysis and intelligent decision-making systems. Graduates design and operate computer systems for analyzing, forecasting, and managing dynamic processes in macroeconomics, technology, environment, and finance. They find employment in various sectors, such as banking, stock markets, ministries, tax agencies, and more, including roles in special services, pension funds, database design, and automated system development.
